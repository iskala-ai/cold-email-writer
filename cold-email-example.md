# cold-email-writer — Worked Example

A complete end-to-end run showing all 19 output sections. Use this as the reference for what "done" looks like.

**Scenario:** an outbound agency in Alexandria targeting a logistics/fulfilment company in Dubai.

---

## The input

> "I need a cold email to the VP of Commercial at a 300-person fulfilment company in Dubai. I sell outbound-as-a-service — we build and run cold email + LinkedIn for B2B clients, around $5k/month. What I know: they just announced a new warehouse in Riyadh, and their careers page has two BD roles open. Best proof I have: one client, a SaaS company, went from 2 to 11 meetings a month in 90 days. I have permission to name them."

## Clarifying questions asked first

> Three, and the second one changes what the email can claim:
>
> 1. **The Riyadh warehouse — announced publicly, and when?** A dated public announcement is a usable opening. Something you inferred from a job posting isn't.
> 2. **Is the 2→11 result from a logistics client or the SaaS client only?** If it's SaaS-only, the email can still use it — but it can't imply it's typical for logistics, and I'll write it accordingly.
> 3. **Who's sending — you as founder, or an SDR?** A VP of Commercial replies to a founder at a materially different rate, and seniority changes what the email can credibly assert.

**Answers given:** (1) Public press release, 5 weeks ago. (2) SaaS client only. (3) Founder.

# Cold Email — VP Commercial · Fulfilment (Dubai)

_Recipient: VP Commercial, ~300 people, UAE · Sender: founder_
_Angle: Riyadh expansion · Built: [date]_

## 1. Executive summary

Cold first-touch to a VP of Commercial whose company publicly announced a Riyadh facility five weeks ago and is hiring two BD roles. The email opens on the expansion — dated, public, and clearly his remit — and names the problem as pipeline needing to exist in a new market before the BD hires land. The claim is deliberately downgraded: one SaaS client's result, named, presented as one case rather than a pattern, because there is no logistics proof. CTA is an interest question, not a calendar ask.

## 3. Audience & context

**Known (sourced):** ~300 employees · fulfilment/logistics · UAE-based · public announcement of a Riyadh facility, 5 weeks ago · two BD roles listed on careers page.

**Assumed:** `[assumption]` The Riyadh facility requires new commercial pipeline in-market rather than being served by existing UAE accounts. Softened in the copy to a hypothesis, not asserted.

**Unknown:** current outbound capability · whether an agency is already engaged · whether the BD roles are for KSA or UAE.

## 4. Emails by committee role

| Role | Email written? | Angle | Ask |
|---|---|---|---|
| **VP Commercial** (decision maker here) | **Primary** | Expansion needs pipeline before hires land | Interest question |
| Founder / MD (economic buyer) | Held | Only if VP refers upward | — |
| BD Manager (end user) | Optional later | The daily prospecting load | Conversation |
| Ops / IT | Not applicable | — | — |

**Multi-thread note:** at ~$5k/month the VP can likely decide or strongly influence. Single-thread first; multi-thread only if no reply after the sequence completes — three simultaneous emails into one commercial team invites comparison.

## 5. What they own

Revenue for the region, and the commercial ramp of a new market. The email must sit inside that remit — an email about "sales efficiency" in the abstract would land as vendor noise.

## 6. Metric & claim

| Metric | Claim as written | Evidence | Strength | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Meetings per month | "One client went from 2 to 11 meetings a month in 90 days" | Named SaaS client, measured | Single case, different industry | **Use — as one case, named, not as typical** |

**Rejected phrasings:**
- ~~"Clients typically see 5x more meetings"~~ — n=1. Fabrication.
- ~~"We help logistics companies scale pipeline"~~ — no logistics proof exists.
- ~~"Proven results across the region"~~ — unsupported and unverifiable.

## 7. Outcome framing

**Personal:** hitting the Riyadh number without waiting on two hires to ramp.
**Business:** pipeline in a new market starting before headcount, not after.

Personal outcome stays implicit — a founder-to-VP email that spells out "you'll look good" reads badly. The business framing carries it.

## 8. Problem line

**Draft:** *"Usually the hard part isn't finding BD people for a new market — it's that they land with no pipeline to walk into."*

**Test — would a non-prospect nod at this?**
No. A company with no expansion and no open BD roles wouldn't recognize it. **Passes.**

**Language check:** "pipeline to walk into" is operator language, not vendor language. He'd say something like this. "Go-to-market enablement" he would not.

## 9. Relevance & stack framing

Doesn't reference any tooling. He may already run outbound in the UAE — the email is deliberately framed around **the new market**, not around a claim that his current setup is broken. Telling a VP his existing motion is inadequate, in a first email, on no evidence, is the fastest route to a delete.

## 10. Reason for reaching out now

**Trigger:** public Riyadh announcement, 5 weeks ago. Dated, verifiable, his remit.

**Freshness:** 5 weeks is inside the useful window for an expansion trigger — recent enough to be live, old enough that hiring is underway. At 6+ months this angle would be stale and would need replacing.

## 11. Email by awareness stage

| Stage | Reading | Adjustment |
|---|---|---|
| **Problem-aware** (assumed) | Knows the ramp is a challenge | Primary draft as written |
| **Solution-aware** | Already evaluating agencies | Variant would lead with the differentiation line, not the problem |
| **Unaware** | Believes existing team covers it | Variant would open on the timing gap between hire and pipeline |

Primary is written for problem-aware, which is the highest-probability reading five weeks after an expansion announcement.

## 12. Differentiation line

**Included:** *"so the pipeline starts before the hires do."*

One clause. Not a positioning paragraph. It states the sequencing advantage without claiming category leadership, which the evidence wouldn't support.

## 13. Objection pre-empt

| Expected objection | Pre-empted? | How |
|---|---|---|
| "We have a team for this" | Yes, implicitly | Framed as *before/alongside* the hires, not instead of them |
| "You don't know logistics" | **No — deliberately** | Naming it in a first email raises a doubt he may not have. Handle on reply. |
| "How did you get my email?" | No | Not warranted for a compliant business email with identity and opt-out |

## 14. Drafts

### Primary

**Subject:** riyadh site

> Hi [First name],
>
> Saw the Riyadh facility announcement last month — and the two BD roles open.
>
> Usually the hard part isn't finding BD people for a new market, it's that they land with no pipeline to walk into. We build and run that part — cold email and LinkedIn — so the pipeline starts before the hires do.
>
> One client, [Named client], went from 2 to 11 meetings a month in 90 days. Different industry, so I'd want to be honest about what transfers.
>
> Worth a look at whether it'd apply to Riyadh?
>
> [Founder name], [Title]
> [Company] · [Address] · [Unsubscribe]

**78 words.**

**Why it's built this way:**
- Opens on a dated public fact in his remit — no flattery, no "hope you're well"
- The inference is softened with *"usually"* — a hypothesis, not a claim about his hiring
- The claim names the client (permission confirmed) **and volunteers the industry mismatch**. Conceding the weakness before he spots it converts a doubt into a credibility signal — and a VP will spot it anyway
- CTA asks for a judgement, not a calendar slot
- No link, no attachment, plain text

### Variant A — cost-of-waiting angle

Same trigger, different problem framing: the months between announcement and first revenue, rather than the hires landing without pipeline. **Tests a different hypothesis, not different words.**

### Variant B — no-trigger control

Written without the Riyadh reference, using only the open BD roles. Measures what the expansion research is actually worth. **If A and B perform identically, the research isn't paying for itself** and the whole list can drop to a cheaper personalization tier.

## 19. Test plan & recommended actions

**Primary metric:** positive reply rate. **Diagnostic:** total reply rate. **Not used:** open rate.

| Test | Compares | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Primary vs Variant A | Two problem framings | Which pain is real |
| Primary vs **Variant B** | Trigger vs no trigger | **What the research is worth** |

⚠️ **Sample-size warning:** at typical B2B reply rates, a list of a few hundred cannot distinguish these variants statistically. Read the **reply content**, not the rate difference, and treat any conclusion as directional. See `ab-test-generator` for the sample-size math before declaring a winner.

## What this example demonstrates

1. **Claim downgrading in practice** — the strongest available proof was a different industry, so the email names the client *and volunteers the mismatch* rather than hiding it.
2. **The problem-line test** — "would a non-prospect nod at this?" is what keeps the opening from being generic.
3. **The no-trigger control** — Variant B exists to measure whether the research is worth its cost, not to test wording.
4. **Fallbacks that reroute rather than degrade** — an empty variable switches the email, it doesn't produce a vaguer sentence.
5. **Honest sample-size caveat** — the skill sets up the test *and* warns that the result probably won't be conclusive at this volume.
